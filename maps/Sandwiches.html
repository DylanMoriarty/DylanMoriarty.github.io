/ Cartography / Madison Sandwich Shops

Sandwich shops have really taken off in the downtown Madison area. Subway encircles the UW-Madison Campus, and there's a good two blocks of State Street where you can get the same food from eight separate restaurants.

With so many options, I've gone ahead and thrown together a tool for you sandwich enthusiasts out there. Just hover over a location and you can see the nearest place you can grab a sub.

The impetus for this project came about from realizing how close together the downtown Subway's are from one another. In one area, you can walk across two streets and find another one. If you're on campus and want that franchise taste, they've got total dominance.

Supposedly this is because Subway is on the cheaper side of franchises to own. I find this explanation the most plausible, because their sandwiches are weird. I can't think of another sub shop where eating their sandwiches makes you smell like their bread for hours afterwards. Supposedly this is a happy accident, but I find it really strange and undesirable.

Because we've seen two new Sub shops pop up in the last year full of excitement to get in on the lucrative world of Submarine Sandwiches, I've prepared a "Sub Shop Island" map to help those intrepid entrepreneurs determine the best location for business.

Using arbitrary distances, areas in red are where people will have to walk the furthest to get their feed of subs (ignoring Jimmy John's whole delivery emphasis for convenience sake).

After all, if you're going to replace a lovely unique business (Dobra Tea *cough*), might as well do it in an area where the same products aren't offered two doors down.
